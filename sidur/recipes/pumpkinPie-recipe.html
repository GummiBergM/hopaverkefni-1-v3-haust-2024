Pumpkin pie

As you pour the first glass of your favorite Chianti or Chardonnay

Easy

recipe

6

people

1

hour

Pumpkin pie is a sweet dessert pie with a spiced, pumpkin-based custard filling. The pumpkin is a symbol of harvest time, and pumpkin pie is often eaten during the fall and early winter.

Ingredients

For the dough

1 tbs of water
2 cups of flour
2 eggs
1 cup of sugar

For the filling

1 tbs of water
2 cups of flour
2 eggs
1 cup of sugar

The method

Wrap the crackers in a tea towel and smash up until fine, breaking up any big bits with your hands, and put them into a large bowl.

Finely chop the parsely, including the stalks. Add the parsley, mustard, fi using, and minced beef to the bowl. Crack in the egg and add a good pinch of salt and pepper.

With clean hands, scrunch and mix everything up well. Divide into 6 and pat and mould each piece into a roundish shape about 2cm thick. Drizzle the burgers with oil, put on a plate, cover and place in the fridge until needed (this helps them to firm up).

With clean hands, scrunch and mix everything up well. Divide into 6 and pat and mould each piece into a roundish shape about 2cm thick. Drizzle the burgers with oil, put on a plate, cover and place in the fridge until needed (this helps them to firm up).

Preheat a large griddle or frying pan for about 4 minutes on a high heat. Turn the heat down to medium.

Place the burgers on the griddle or in the pan and use a spatula ot lightly press down on them, making sure the burger is in full ocntact. Cook them ot your liking for 3 or 4 minutes on each side - you may need to cook them in two batches.

Wash and dry a few small lettuce leaves, tearing up the larger ones. Slice the omatoes. Peel and finely slice the red onion. Slice the gherkins lengthways as finely as you can. Place all this on a platter and put in the middle of the table with plates, cutlery, ketchup and drinks.

remove your burgers to another plate and carefully wipe your pan or griddle clean with kitchen paper.

Halve your burger buns and lightly toast them on the griddle or in the pan. Also great with a chopped salad.
